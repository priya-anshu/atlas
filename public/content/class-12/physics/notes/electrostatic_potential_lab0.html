Special Cases

● q₁q₂ < 0 → U negative (attractive)
● r → ∞ → U → 0 (reference point)

Dipole Electric Potential

Potential at P depends on distance r AND angle θ with dipole axis.

V = kp·cosθ / r²
Key Special Cases
θ=0° (Axial): V = +kp/r² (max positive)
θ=180°: V = −kp/r² (max negative)
θ=90° (Equatorial): V = 0 always!
V ∝ 1/r² (faster than point charge 1/r)

Equipotential Surfaces

Every point on the surface has the same electric potential.

W = q(V_A − V_B) = 0
Properties
① No work moving charge along surface
② E always ⊥ to equipotential surface
③ Two surfaces NEVER intersect
④ Closer spacing → Stronger E field
⑤ Conductor surface is equipotential
Configuration
Concentric spheres. Closer spacing near charge = stronger E field.

E–V Relation

E field = negative gradient of potential. Points from high→low V.

E = −dV/dx
E⃗ = −∇V
V_A − V_B = ∫E·dr
Key Insights
● E in direction of decreasing V
● Steep V gradient → Strong E
● V=const → E ⊥ surface
● Unit: E in V/m = N/C
