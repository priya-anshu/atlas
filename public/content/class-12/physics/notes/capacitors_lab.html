Capacitance

Ability to store charge per unit potential difference.

C = Q / V
Q = CV
V = Q / C
Units & Scale
SI unit: Farad (F) = coulomb/volt
1 μF = 10⁻⁶ F  |  1 nF = 10⁻⁹ F  |  1 pF = 10⁻¹² F
Capacitance depends on shape, size, separation of conductors and medium between them.

Parallel Plate Capacitor

Two conducting plates of area A separated by distance d.

C = ε₀A / d
E = σ/ε₀ = Q/(ε₀A)
V = Ed = Qd/(ε₀A)
To Increase Capacitance
① Increase plate area A ↑
② Decrease separation d ↓
③ Insert dielectric (K > 1)

Dielectric Effect

Inserting a dielectric changes C, E, V, and U differently depending on whether battery is connected.

C = ε₀KA/d = KC₀
Two Cases
🔋 Battery Connected
V = constant
C → KC
Q → KQ
E = constant
U → KU (increases)
🔌 Battery Disconnected
Q = constant
C → KC
V → V/K
E → E/K
U → U/K (decreases)

Common Potential

When two charged capacitors are connected, charge flows until potentials equalise.

V_common = (C₁V₁ + C₂V₂)/(C₁ + C₂)
ΔU = C₁C₂(V₁−V₂)²/2(C₁+C₂)
Key Points
● Charge is conserved: Q₁+Q₂ = Q₁'+Q₂'
● Energy is always lost (heat in wire)
● Loss = 0 only if V₁ = V₂
● Potential lost ≠ Potential gained (capacities differ)

Properties of Conductors
① E_inside = 0 (static condition)
② E at surface = σ/ε₀ (normal to surface)
③ No excess charge inside volume
④ V = constant throughout
⑤ Surface charge density σ varies by curvature
⑥ High σ at sharp points → E large → discharge
